What you should see if the rope works

1. Sag: rope hangs as a smooth catenary curve between the two anchors. Settles in <2 seconds, doesn't oscillate forever. Stretches no more than the budget allows.
2. Pendulum: blob swings under gravity, rope stays mostly taut (anchored end at top, free blob at bottom). Period should look reasonable; no infinite acceleration; doesn't snap apart on hard swings.
3. Over peg: rope routes around the peg cleanly, blob hangs on one side, weight transfers through the peg without the rope clipping through it.
4. Two blobs over peg: two free soft bodies connected by a rope, draped over a peg. Neither is anchored — the rope alone holds them. They should reach a hanging equilibrium where the heavier (or lower-starting) one pulls the other up over the peg. Bumping the system (tweak `seed` or restart) should change which one settles lower.
